TripPal

Optimizing the travel planning experience

mobile design, travel app
Duration 4 weeks
Tools Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator

Project Scope

TripPal is an app designed for users who identify themselves as frequent travelers. Through user research and analysis, I designed this concept project to improve the overall user experience when traveling.

The Issue

Through user research, it was found that those who travel month to month experience pain points in organizing their travel plans. Users have to navigate between multiple apps or go through a number of emails before finding their reservations.

The Solution

By creating a centralized app that can sync all reservation emails, boarding passes and confirmations, it will help users travel with ease.

Research Results

Combined with interviews and survey, users mentioned the ease if they didn't have to go through multiple apps. Users wished they can see cancellation policies, carry-on fees and other important details about their reservations in one centralized app. Users found frustration when having to look through emails, especially if the reservation was booked months in advance.

With the information gathered from the interviews, I constructed an affinity diagram to better see our target audience. After organizing each profile, I created categories such as:

  • What apps and websites they use
  • What their methods of trip planning are
  • Their pain points
  • Their goals

User Personas

From data gathered and analyzed from user interviews and surveys, two user personas were developed to represent the population.

Tiffany represents those who travel frequently for leisure, while Andrew represents those who travels for work. Both personas benefit from the organizational methods in TripPal. Although their pain points and goals vary, TripPal aims to meet both of their needs to create an overall seamless app.

Feature Analysis

Taking the most used online resources by Tiffany and Andrew, I conducted a feature analysis between 5 competitors. The features here are ones that Tiffany and Andrew found useful while using a trip planning app, or things they wished they had.

This helps us understand where the current product stands in the market, and to know the strengths and weaknesses of the competition.

Task Flows

I created a user flow for the predetermined tasks that were completed by participants during user testing. The tasks were:

Task 1: Create a personalized trip
  Subtask 1: Check to make sure the dates are right
  Subtask 2: View email reservations that fall under the   timeline of travel be added to trip

Task 2: Find possible activities around the area of travel
  Subtask 1: Add them onto their trip

Feedback

When gathering feedback on which features to use for the best user experience, I decided on conducting an A/B Testing.

  • Users preferred seeing a vertical scroll versus horizontal scroll of items on individual trip pages
  • Users expressed positively to having photos with their itineraries as opposed to a blank list
